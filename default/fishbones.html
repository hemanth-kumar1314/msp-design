Fish Bones

Fish Bones are a significant marine co-product, serving as a rich, natural source of Calcium Phosphate (Hydroxyapatite) and residual Collagen/Gelatin. We process the skeletal structures of various fish species into versatile raw materials for industrial use.

These bones are meticulously cleaned and dried to produce a contaminant-free ingredient. Their composition, particularly the presence of highly bioavailable Calcium and Phosphorus, makes them crucial for synthesizing mineral supplements and specialized biochemicals.

MSP ensures that our fish bones are sourced sustainably and processed rapidly to preserve the quality of the organic and mineral fractions. They are primarily used in the production of animal feed supplements and advanced biomaterials.

Applications & Key Benefits

Dicalcium Phosphate (DCP) Production

The main raw material for manufacturing premium Dicalcium Phosphate (DCP), an essential, bioavailable mineral supplement for livestock and aquaculture feeds.

Marine Hydroxyapatite Source

A natural, highly pure source of Hydroxyapatite, used in biomedical applications such as bone graft substitutes and dental research due to its superior compatibility.

Natural Gelatin & Collagen Base

Processed fish bones are utilized for the extraction of high-quality Gelatin and Collagen, serving various markets from food to pharmaceuticals.

Nutrient-Rich Feed Ingredient

Can be milled into bone meal for direct inclusion in animal and pet feeds, providing a natural, holistic blend of protein, calcium, and phosphorus.
