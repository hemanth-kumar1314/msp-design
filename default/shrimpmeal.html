Shrimp Meal

Shrimp meal is a good ingredient for aquaculture feed because of its high crude protein content and digestibility. It is rich in lysine and Methionine, highly unsaturated fatty acids, and is a good source of Choline, an essential nutrient.

The calcium content depends on the number of shells in the product. It is not only high in protein but also low in calories, carbs, and fat. We sustainably upcycle approximately 70% of total shrimp landings that would otherwise become waste.

A portion of the crude protein is in the form of Chitin. Using shrimp meal as a feedstuff is a key part of waste management in the shrimp industry.

Applications & Key Benefits

Aquaculture Excellence

Primarily used as a valuable fish feed for aquaculture, ensuring high crude protein and optimal digestibility.

Growth Promotion

The Chitin content in shrimp meals is believed to have a significant growth-promoting effect on farmed fish and shrimp.

Nutrient Density

Rich source of essential nutrients like lysine, Methionine, and Choline, and high in unsaturated fatty acids.

Sustainability

Utilizing shrimp meal contributes directly to waste management in the shrimp industry and supports a circular economy.
